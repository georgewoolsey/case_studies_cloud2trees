1 Introduction

The integration of modern precision forestry tools promotes data-driven solutions to advance sustainable outcomes across forest planning and management implementation. While advanced inventories utilizing aerial point cloud data from airborne laser scanning (ALS) and digital aerial photogrammetry (DAP) have been developed and validated for over two decades, their adoption by the land management industry remains limited by technical barriers. Individual tree detection (ITD) data processing routines are siloed across isolated research papers and disparate software packages, resulting in a steep analytical burden for analysts and managers to implement these routines. This burden consumes time and increases the potential for implementation errors. Moving beyond theoretical method development and refinement of ITD approaches to active operational implementation is needed to truly assess the practical utility and limitations of existing methods and identify factors that matter most for real-world management which may be understudied. The cloud2trees R package bridges this gap by compiling these fragmented, multi-stage processing routines into a unified, open-source platform. By removing the technical hurdles required to transform raw aerial point clouds into comprehensive single-tree inventories with complete biophysical attributes (such as height, DBH, and CBH), cloud2trees transitions remote sensing from a theoretical academic exercise into an accessible tool for active land management.

1.1 Objective

The primary objective of this framework is to move beyond theoretical method development and establish a standardized, scalable pipeline that translates raw aerial point cloud data into familiar tree list inventories for use in site-specific, spatially-informed silvicultural prescription development. Forest managers are tasked with “increasing the pace and scale” of active treatments to confront escalating wildfire activity and rising wood demand with a shrinking workforce. Here, we use the cloud2trees framework to demonstrate a precision forestry approach by executing end-to-end processing workflows for two real-world operational challenges:

  1. QUIC-Fire Input Generation: use raw point clouds to generate spatial fuel data (bulk density and canopy base height) for use as input into the coupled fire-atmosphere QUIC-fire model. Prescribed fire modeling enables managers to optimize prescribed burn plans for effective implementation and safety.

  2. Individual, Clumps, and Openings (ICO) Thinning Prescription: use raw point clouds to generate spatial tree lists required to plan and execute structural restoration treatments following the Churchill et al. (2016) methodology, providing a clear, reproducible cut-retention list based on target basal area constraints and specific overstory clump proportions.

cloud2trees standardizes point cloud processing workflows into a single-command operation to empower land managers and interdisciplinary scientists to focus entirely on implementing precision forestry on the ground where it impacts ecosystem resilience and wildfire risk reduction.
